Greener

EaS-E not only has zero tail pipe emissions, but it is truly sustainable from the over lifecycle carbon emissions perspective.

With almost the lowest KWh/Mile consumed by the EaS-E, the carbon emissions linked to the electricity generation are kept at a minimal compared to any other EV on the market.

To give you a number, the best selling EV cars consumes about 0.25-0.35KWh/Mile, where as the EaS-E will have a less than half of it at around .1-.125 Kwh/Mile. So the EaS-E is more than twice as green compared to an average EV car on the American roads.

Also the battery pack manufacturing process is carbon intensive & it contributes largely to the over all lifecycle carbon emissions of the EV car. In most EV cars the battery pack sizes are around 30-60 Kwhrs, as against 10-12 KWhrs in the EaS-E.
